KHEER

Indulge in the rich and creamy delight of Kheer – a classic Indian rice pudding infused with aromatic spices , simmered to perfection in milk, and sweetened with a touch of sugar. A comforting dessert that captures the essence of traditional flavors in every spoonful

RECIPE

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts-almonds, pistachios, or cashews
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder (for color)
  • 1 teaspoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • Raisins for garnish (optional)

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. Wash the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat the ghee over medium heat. Add the drained rice and sauté for 2-3 minutes until the rice turns slightly translucent.
  3. Pour in the whole milk and bring to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat to low and let the rice simmer, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. If using saffron, soak the saffron strands in a tablespoon of warm milk. After a few minutes, add the saffron milk to the rice mixture.
  5. Continue simmering the rice in milk, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens. This may take about 30-40 minutes.
  6. Add the sugar and cardamom powder to the rice mixture. Stir well to combine and let it simmer for another 10-15 minutes until the sugar dissolves and the kheer thickens further.
  7. In a separate small pan, lightly roast the chopped nuts until they become fragrant. Keep a close eye on them to prevent burning.
  8. Once the kheer reaches your desired consistency, turn off the heat. Stir in the roasted nuts.
  9. Allow the kheer to cool slightly before serving. It will continue to thicken as it cools.
  10. Serve the kheer warm or chilled, garnished with more chopped nuts and raisins if desired.
Enjoy the comforting and delicious Indian dessert, Kheer, with its blend of creamy rice, aromatic cardamom, and delightful nutty crunch!
