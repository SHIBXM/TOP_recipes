CHILLI Chicken

Indulge in the aromatic delight of Tandoori Chicken, a classic Indian dish that boasts tender marinated chicken, infused with a harmonious blend of yogurt, spices, and herbs. Roasted to perfection in a tandoor or oven, this succulent dish offers a delightful balance of smoky flavors and mouthwatering tenderness. Elevate your culinary journey with this iconic recipe that captures the heart of Indian cuisine.

RECIPE

INGREDIENTS:

  • 4 chicken leg quarters, skinless
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons tandoori masala spice mix
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder (for color)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

INSTRUCTIONS:

  1. Make deep cuts in the chicken leg quarters to allow the marinade to penetrate. This helps the flavors infuse better
  2. In a bowl, mix together the yogurt, tandoori masala spice mix, ginger paste, garlic paste, lemon juice, cumin powder, paprika or red chili powder, and salt. Adjust the spice level to your preference.
  3. Coat the chicken leg quarters with the marinade, making sure to get the mixture into the cuts you made earlier. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2-4 hours, or preferably overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and the chicken to tenderize.
  4. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). If using a tandoor, preheat it according to its instructions.
  5. Place a wire rack on a baking sheet and lightly grease it. This will prevent the chicken from sticking.
  6. Arrange the marinated chicken leg quarters on the wire rack, allowing any excess marinade to drip off.
  7. Bake the chicken in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. You can also finish by broiling for an additional 2-3 minutes to get a slightly charred effect.
  8. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve the Tandoori Chicken hot with lemon wedges on the side.
  10. Enjoy your homemade Tandoori Chicken as an appetizer or main course, accompanied by naan bread, rice, or a refreshing side salad.
NOTE:If you have access to a grill, you can also grill the marinated chicken over medium-high heat until cooked through and slightly charred, which adds an extra layer of smoky flavor.
